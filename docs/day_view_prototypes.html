Option A · Embedded + inline signals
Pros: Cleanest. Six distinct components on screen (week strip, day brief, weather, Now, Next, Later). Travel and OOO live inside the timeline at their actual times. Deltas and conflicts attach inline to the affected card — small badges next to the title.

Cons: A multi-day trip or whole-day OOO has nowhere "above the timeline" to surface — they only appear at the moment a leg starts. Conflict resolution lives inside the meeting card; no top-level "things to fix" affordance.

Option D · Inline + attention strip
Pros: Same minimalism as A (all items embedded in Now/Next/Later) but adds ONE thing: a single thin attention strip at the top that counts "things to look at" with a direct jump. Best of both — clean default, single bar that elevates when there's actual work. Whole-day signals (trip, OOO) still surface in the week strip's markers.

Cons: The attention strip can feel redundant on calm days (it just says "nothing to look at"). If we hide it on calm days, the layout shifts — David can't predict whether the strip will be there. Inline badges still do most of the work; the strip is a small addition.
