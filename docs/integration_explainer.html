A kitchen story

Why your daily dossier isn't getting fresh data

Imagine BoringOS is a big shared kitchen, and Executive Assistant is a new chef who joined to make one specific dish every morning: your daily dossier. The dish needs fresh ingredients — your calendar and your email. Right now those ingredients aren't arriving, and six different things are quietly broken. Here's the story.

The cast

Before the story, meet everyone in the kitchen:

🏢 BoringOSThe kitchen itself. Has equipment, staff, and a bell system everyone shares.
👨‍🍳 Executive Assistant (Chef EA)The new chef who just joined. Wants to make the “daily dossier” dish every morning.
📅 Google CalendarThe appointment-book supplier. Drops off what's on your schedule.
📧 GmailThe mail courier. Brings letters all day long.
🗂️ Triage HelperThe kitchen helper who sorts mail into piles: urgent / important / FYI / junk.
📜 WorkflowA recipe card on the wall: “step 1, step 2, step 3.”
⏰ RoutineThe kitchen timer. “Every 15 minutes, do this.”
🔔 EventAn announcement over the kitchen intercom. “New mail just arrived!”

What's supposed to happen

In a working kitchen, the morning goes like this:

  1. ⏰ The kitchen timer dings every 15 minutes. Chef EA's prep station fetches the latest appointments from the calendar supplier and writes them in the appointment book.
  2. 📧 Gmail keeps dropping off mail. Triage Helper sorts each letter and shouts the category over the intercom: “Letter 47 is urgent!”
  3. ✈️ Whenever Triage shouts about something travel-related, Chef EA's assistant (the travel-lens) runs over, reads it, and writes the trip details in a travel log.
  4. ☕ At 6:00 AM the next morning, Chef EA sits down with a fresh appointment book and travel log, and writes the dossier.

What's actually happening

Six things are broken. Let's walk through them.

GAP 1 🔕 The doorbell that never rings Broken in the kitchen

The kitchen has a doorbell labeled “supplier connected!” — it's supposed to ring the moment you sign up with Google. Chef EA wired his timer to start ringing only after that doorbell rings, because there's no point fetching appointments before the supplier is set up.

Problem: nobody ever wired the doorbell. The button is on the wall, but it's not connected to anything. You can sign up with Google a hundred times — no ding, no announcement, nothing.

ResultChef EA's “fetch appointments every 15 minutes” timer is waiting for a doorbell that will never ring. It stays asleep forever.
GAP 2 🤖 The made-up machine Recipe-card typo

Chef EA's recipe card has a step that says “use the Fancy Connector-Fetcher 3000 to grab the appointments.”

Problem: there is no such machine in this kitchen. The kitchen has a regular Fetcher, but not a “Connector-Fetcher 3000”. When the kitchen reads a step that calls for equipment it doesn't have, it just silently skips the step — no error, no warning, the recipe pretends the step happened.

What the recipe says

step: use Connector-Fetcher 3000

What the kitchen has

step: use google.calendar.list_events
ResultEven if the timer rang (it doesn't), the “fetch appointments” step would just be empty.
GAP 3 ✏️ The typo in the recipe Recipe-card typo

The next step on the recipe says “for-each appointment, write it in the book.” But the kitchen only understands “for_each” — underscore, not hyphen. It's like asking for “tom-ato” when the kitchen only knows “tom_ato”.

What the recipe says

for-each

What the kitchen understands

for_each

And there's a second problem here. Chef EA's recipe wrote the loop in two pieces — “first start the loop, then over here is the chopping bit.” But the kitchen's for_each is a one-shot: it does the loop and the per-item work in a single block. Even fixing the typo wouldn't make this work.

ResultEven if everything else worked, the loop would never iterate.
GAP 4 ⏰ The time machine that doesn't exist Recipe-card typo

The recipe says “get appointments from NOW to NOW+30 days.”

But the kitchen has no clock that understands the word “NOW”. The supplier only accepts actual dates and times, like “May 14, 2026 at 8:00 AM.”

What the recipe asks for

timeMin: "NOW"
timeMax: "NOW+30d"

What the supplier needs

timeMin: "2026-05-14T00:00:00Z"
timeMax: "2026-06-13T23:59:59Z"
ResultIf the order ever got placed, the supplier would say “I don't understand 'NOW'” and send back nothing.
GAP 5 🚪 The phantom button Recipe-card typo

The recipe for waking up the timers says “press the un-pause button on the timer.” But there is no “un-pause button” in this kitchen. There's a regular set of timer controls, but not that one specific button Chef EA wrote down.

ResultEven if the doorbell rang (Gap 1), the recipe couldn't actually flip the timer on. It would just stand there pressing a button that doesn't exist.
GAP 6 📦 The wrong shelf Recipe-card typo

The supplier delivers a box. The recipe says “take the cookies from the box.” But the supplier doesn't put cookies directly in the box — they put them in a tin inside the box. The recipe reaches in, doesn't find cookies, and grabs nothing.

What the recipe grabs

box.cookies → nothing

Where the cookies are

box.data.cookies → the cookies!
ResultThe loop runs over an empty list.

What's surprisingly still working

Not everything is broken. Two parts work fine today:

OK ☕ The 6 AM dossier writing Working

Chef EA's morning routine — sitting down at 6 AM, reading the appointment book, writing the dossier — doesn't go through any of the broken recipe cards. It just reads whatever's in the book and writes from that.

So technically Chef EA is composing every morning… from an empty appointment book. Like a writer with a blank page.

OK ✈️ Travel-lens on the intercom Working

When Triage Helper sorts a piece of mail and shouts the category over the intercom, Chef EA's travel-lens assistant correctly hears it. If the category sounds travel-related, the assistant runs over and reads the letter. This path works end to end.

Three ways to fix it

Now that we know what's broken, here are the options. You pick.

🍳 Option A — Build a simple prep station Recommended

Forget the complicated recipe cards. Give Chef EA his own small prep station that knows two things:

  1. Ask the supplier for today's appointments (using real dates, not the word “NOW”).
  2. Put each appointment in the appointment book.

The timer just says “every 15 min, run the prep station.” No recipe cards, no doorbell, no phantom buttons. We throw out the three broken recipes entirely.

Sidesteps all six gaps — no doorbell, no typos, no shelves, no NOW.

Easy to test on a laptop without a real kitchen connection.

Doesn't need anything fixed inside BoringOS.

A bit more EA-side code to write upfront (~150 lines + tests).

📝 Option B — Fix every typo on the recipe cards

Keep the recipe-card system. Fix all six problems individually: rename the machine, fix the hyphen, compute real dates somehow, find or invent the un-pause button, fix the wrong shelf — and ask the kitchen owner to wire up the doorbell.

Keeps the current architecture untouched.

Six separate fixes, all fragile.

Depends on fixing something inside BoringOS (Gap 1).

Hard to test — recipe cards aren't easy to run on a laptop.

⚙️ Option C — Option A + fix the kitchen's doorbell

Do option A (the prep station), and patch the kitchen so the doorbell actually rings when a supplier connects. The doorbell fix is one line of code in BoringOS but it's in a different repo, so we'd cross a boundary.

Future chefs in this kitchen will get a working doorbell.

Touches two repos. The workspace rules say do the framework first, then the consumer — so a bit more careful work.

Bonus question: when should the timer start?

▶️ Start the timer immediately Recommended

The timer starts the moment Chef EA is hired. If you haven't connected Google yet, the prep station just shrugs and tries again in 15 minutes — like a coffee maker that politely says “no water yet, I'll check again soon.”

Zero manual setup. The moment you OAuth Google, the next 15-min tick picks it up.

A little harmless logging while it waits.

🛑 Manually flip the timer on

Timer stays off until you go into the admin UI and switch it on yourself, after you've connected Google.

No noise in logs.

One more thing for you to remember.

Bonus question: how far ahead should we look?

📅 Today + 7 days Recommended

A week's worth of appointments. Enough to notice “the Wednesday meeting got moved to Thursday” but not so much you're fetching things you don't need.

📆 Today + 30 days

A whole month. Helpful if you're planning trips far in advance. More work each tick; lots of repeat data.

🌅 Today only

Minimal. Just today's appointments. Misses anything that shifts tomorrow before tomorrow morning.
